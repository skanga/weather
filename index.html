About Weather

Weather forecasts without ads, cookies, or tracking.

Most weather websites bury the forecast under autoplay video, popups, banners, third-party tracking pixels, and cookie-consent dialogs. Weather is the opposite of that: open the page, see the weather, leave.

  • No ads. No display ads, native ads, sponsored content, or affiliate links.
  • No tracking. No analytics, pixels, fingerprinting, or third-party scripts.
  • No cookies. Preferences stay in your browser's local storage.
  • Self-hosted fonts. Fonts come from this site, not Google Fonts.
  • Open source. The source code is on GitHub.

Weather is a static site. There is no backend user database because there are no users. Your browser calls weather services directly.

Privacy Policy

No cookies. No tracking. No ads.

This site does not use cookies, does not track your location, and does not collect any personal information. We don't use analytics, fingerprinting, or any form of user tracking.

When you search for a city or postal code, that search text is sent for lookup. When you view weather or use your current location, coordinates are sent to the following third-party services to retrieve weather data:

  • Open-Meteo - city geocoding, weather forecasts, and air quality data (called directly from your browser)
  • National Weather Service (NWS) - US location labels and severe weather alerts (called directly)
  • RainViewer & CartoDB — radar imagery and map tiles (called directly)
  • NoAdsRadar — our own future-cast radar service for the contiguous United States. Routed through Cloud Run. Receives only the tile coordinates needed for display. Our code does not log IPs or user identifiers, and Google Cloud logging exclusions are configured so the platform's default access logs are not retained.
  • Zippopotam — postal/zip code to coordinates lookup (60+ countries, called directly)
  • OpenWeatherMap — severe weather alerts for non-US locations. Called directly from your browser only if you add your own One Call API key in Settings. Your coordinates and API key are sent to OpenWeatherMap.
  • Google Translate — only if you click the "Translate" link next to a weather alert. The alert text and your selected UI language are sent to Google Translate via a new tab. We don't send anything to Google unless you choose to translate.

Your preferences (dark mode, temperature units, time format, language, visual style, section layout, and optional OpenWeatherMap key) are saved in your browser's local storage (not a cookie) so they persist between visits. You can clear all of this anytime by clearing your browser data.

Your phone might ask you to "Install this page", which means creating a shortcut to a website on your home screen, known as a Progressive Web App (PWA). This makes the site act like a native app, allowing it to load faster, and appear with your other apps on your home screen.

This site is open source. No data is sold, shared, or monetized in any way.
